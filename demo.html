What are Foundational Models?

Foundational models are massive, general-purpose AI systems trained on vast and diverse datasets. They aren't built for just one task; instead, they serve as a powerful, adaptable base that can be fine-tuned for a wide array of specialized applications, from writing poetry to analyzing scientific data.

How Transformers Work: The Engine of Modern AI

The Transformer architecture revolutionized AI by processing entire sequences of data at once. Its key innovation is the "self-attention" mechanism, which allows the model to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence, capturing complex context and relationships to generate incredibly human-like text.

Fine-Tuning & Evaluation

To perform specific tasks, general foundational models must be specialized. This "fine-tuning" process adapts the model to a narrower domain, like medicine or law. Afterward, rigorous evaluation ensures the model is accurate, coherent, and free of errors or "hallucinations."

Fine-Tuning Approaches

Full Fine-Tuning

Trains all model parameters on new data. Highly effective but resource-intensive.

PEFT (Parameter-Efficient)

Trains only a small subset of parameters. Much faster and more efficient.

Balanced Evaluation
